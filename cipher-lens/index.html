Desktop YouTube Intelligence

Cipher Lens turns transcripts into strategy, briefs, and publish-ready outputs.

Cipher Lens is a local-first desktop workspace for creators and researchers who want more than a plain summary. It pulls YouTube transcripts, cleans caption noise, builds export packs, and runs channel deep dives and compare workflows in one app.

What it does

Analyze single videos, generate creator briefs, export repurposing packs, and compare full channels with saved reports.

Built with

Electron, React, TypeScript, Vite, Tesseract.js, and a local-first desktop workflow designed for real shipping use.

Best for

YouTube workflows, creator operations, transcript research, channel audits, and client-ready PDF deck generation.

Core Workflow

1. Bring in the source

Paste a transcript, import subtitle files, or fetch public YouTube captions and metadata.

2. Clean the transcript

Trim fillers, remove noise, dedupe caption lines, and normalize the text before analysis.

3. Generate outputs

Create summaries, planner views, chapter packs, clip moments, and platform-specific export content.

4. Save and reuse

Keep briefs, reports, and presets in the built-in library and export text or PDF deliverables.

Why it matters

More than summary tools

Cipher Lens is aimed at actual content and research workflows, not just one-click transcript condensation.

Local-first desktop behavior

The app stays in a practical desktop workflow with imports, save dialogs, exports, and reusable history.
